About me


I was born and raised in a small town (Zanesville, OH) with a close-knit community where I developed a love for the outdoors and physical activity. I am now living in Cincinnati, Ohio since becoming a full-time student at the University of Cincinnati. Some of my hobbies include lifting weights, playing basketball, and most of all enjoying quality time with friends and family.

I was a very active person from a young age and spent a lot of time outside and playing sports. I am still an avid sports fan and I am always finding ways to stay in shape. I was lucky to have such a supportive family and friends who encouraged my interests and passions. I put a lot of effort toward self-improvement, and I am always seeking out new challenges and opportunities to grow. With my experience in sports and blue-collar work along with my supportive family, I know that my strong work ethic and determination will continue to drive me to succeed.

Since I was young, I have always been inspired to create. I believe that technology is a powerful tool that allows us to bring our ideas to life and make a real impact on the world. This is what drew me to information technology and software development. I have a passion for using technology as a tool to solve problems and improve people's lives, and I believe that there is infinite potential for growth and innovation in this field.


My coding journey


My coding journey started with a passion for design. During my early years in high school, I took a graphic design course out of curiousity. Adobe Photoshop amazed me with its features and use cases. It quicky became a fun hobby that allowed me to express my creativity and create cool things. Over time, I became proficient in Photoshop and my work. My interest and experience in design, my desire for growth and my proficiency with technology had led me to explore programming and development.

Java was the first language that i wrote "Hello, World!" in. I was first introduced to Java and was immediately presented with the logic and structure of Object-Oriented-Programming. I spent a long time learning the basics of writing clean code and making the most out of my simple projects/programs.

As I've progressed in my studies, I became much more interested the front-end with web development. I started to learn front-end languages. I was amazed at how basic HTML and CSS can be used to create functional websites. I was determined to master these technologies, and I've spent countless hours customizing and experimenting with my designs using HTML, CSS, and Javascript.
